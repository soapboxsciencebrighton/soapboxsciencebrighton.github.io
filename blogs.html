At Soapbox Science, we are fortunate to have a diverse group of women presentating a diverse range of science! This blog page allows you to learn more about our Soapbox Science Brighton speakers and their science. Note that all the blogs are are hosted on the Soapbox Science central website.

The power of collaboration and diversity in interdisciplinary research:
the case of a responsible gambling project

Read Ruijie's blog post

Ruijie is an interdisciplinary researcher and Lecturer in Psychology based at Bournemouth University, where her research interests lie in Cyberpsychology and Human Computer Interaction. For Soapbox Science 2022, Ruijie will be talking about “Being 'GambleAware': How science and tech can help and why collaboration matters” on Brighton's seafront.

An expedition into the world of sub-atomic particles

Read Charanjit's blog post

Charanjit Kaur Khosa will be speaking at Soapbox Science Brighton 2022, describing the current questions in theoretical particle physics and the approaches being used to tackle these questions. These methods developed in particle physics can also solve problems in other areas like global warming and epidemiology.

Charanjit is keen to share the joy of science with young students, and to inspire young women to pursue science and push the frontiers of scientific knowledge. At the moment, more women are needed to participate in science to bridge the gender gap in STEM fields, which will also help bring equality in all other facets of society.
