By the i360

Soapbox Science Brighton will be returning to Brighton & Hove beach on Saturday 1st June 2024, and we have now selected speakers for this year's event!

If you are a woman, non-binary or genderqueer person who works in science, who is eager to talk to the general public about your research work in a fun, informal setting, then Soapbox Science Brighton is for you!

We welcome applications from active researchers and innovators in any STEMM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics, Medicine) discipline. We do not accept applications from Undergraduate or Masters students, or group applications, however.

To apply, please complete the online form at the Soapbox Science central website - just choose Brighton as your preferred venue on the form.

The deadline for applications is Thursday 29th February, 2024.

Please advertise this opportunity far and wide using the poster on the right, which is also available as a PDF document.

If you are interested in volunteering to help the event run smoothly, please visit our Soapbox Science Brighton Volunteers page.

Aygun Badalova



Can people with dementia learn names of the people they don't remember?

Presentation slot: 12noon-1pm
Aygun is a PhD student at the University College London with a background in neuropsychology and cognitive neuroscience. Her current research focuses on the development of Digital Interventions in Neuro-Rehabilitation (DINR) of Alzheimer's disease and Dementia. She has joined the lab where she works on the app called the Gotcha! - a name retrieval therapy app to help people with dementia remember the names of familiar people. Aygun is also doing clinical work at the National Hospital for Neurology and Neurosurgery (NHNN).
